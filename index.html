NoiseMap NYC

Predictive Neighbourhood Noise Intelligence for Apartment Renters
Problem Statement & Objectives

Renters typically evaluate neighbourhoods based on a single visit, reflecting only current conditions and failing to capture how noise may evolve over their tenancy period. Prolonged noise exposure exceeding the World Health Organization's recommended threshold of 55 decibels has been associated with chronic health conditions including cardiovascular disease and sleep disturbance. Furthermore, empirical research indicates that noise above this threshold reduces apartment rental values by approximately 2% per unit increase, making this both a public health and financial concern. However, no existing tool forecasts how neighbourhood noise levels will change over a 6 to 12 month horizon or identifies the specific sources driving those changes. This project addresses this gap by utilising publicly available data to predict future neighbourhood noise levels, attribute them to their dominant sources, and deliver findings through a renter-facing interactive map.

Methodology & Features

The system ingests five open data sources — NYC construction permit filings, traffic volume counts, MapPLUTO zoning data, NYC 311 noise complaints, and acoustic readings from the SONYC urban sensor network — spatially and temporally aligned to a standardised 200×200 metre grid by month, producing a unified matrix used to train an XGBoost regression model for monthly noise prediction over a 12-month timeline, incorporating approved future permits and seasonal traffic projections as forward-looking inputs. SHAP is then applied to decompose each prediction into the contribution of each noise source, displayed to users as human-readable badges such as Construction and Traffic. The system delivers a colour-coded prediction map across all grid blocks, a noise timeline chart per grid, a lease overlap calculator highlighting predicted noisy months within a user's tenancy period, and a neighbourhood comparison tool for side-by-side noise profiling of two addresses.
